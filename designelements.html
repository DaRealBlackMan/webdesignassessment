Shape


This is the basic shape of a helmet. The helmet was originally made from a square mesh. The square mesh was subdivided into three cuts. After subdividing the mesh I cut it in half to get the bowl-like shape of a helmet. Once making the bowl-like shape I used the extrude option to extend and create some faces to make the padded neck cover and the ear pads/muffs of the helmet. My original plan for the helmet was to make it look like a racing helmet, so I decided to make the helmet have a neck pad/cover and ear pads to give it a racing kind of look. I made the ear pads stick out to show the general position of a racer's ears so it would look more realistic. The neck and ear pads are lower down the helmet compared to the front of the helmet that
covers the forehead because the neck and ears are lower than the forehead and I modelled the
helmet like this to make it look genuine.



Texture

When I made the basic shape of the helmet I planned on making the texture of it smooth to make it look brand new and shiny/reflective. The helmet started out as a default square mesh which I subdivided and smoothed into three cuts, making it more detailed, smooth and precise. After I made the neck and ear pads I smoothed them out to make the shape more round resulting in the helmet looking glossy compared to it looking ragged and bulky if it wasn't smooth.



Colour


I chose a greyish-black colour as the vital colour of the helmet as it makes the helmet look shiny, crisp and simple. There is a red line on top of the helmet running down the middle of it, making a design. It also makes the helmet look more attractive and eye-catching. The greyish-black shows power and mystery and the red adds to it as it is appealing and it is an accent colour, contrasting well with black.
